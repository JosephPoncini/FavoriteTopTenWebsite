10. Sheep (Tier 3 Pet). The sheep takes number ten because of its ability to summon more sheep works very well with other pets who get bonuses when friends are summoned. Teams can only have a max of 5 pets at a time so be careful that your sheep have room
Level 1 Faint: Summon two 2/2 Ram.
Level 2 Faint: Summon two 4/4 Ram.
Level 3 Faint: Summon two 6/6 Ram.


9. Giraffe (Tier 3 Pet). The Giraffe takes 9 on my list because of its ability to add health to its teammates each round. This allow teammates to start building pretty early on within the game
Level 1: End turn: Give the nearest friend ahead +1 Attack and +1 Health.
Level 2: End turn: Give the nearest 2 friends ahead +1 Attack and +1 Health.
Level 3: End turn: Give the nearest 3 friends ahead +1 Attack and +1 Health.


8. Deer (Tier 4). The deer is a personal favorite of mine. It takes it place on my list because of its ability to summon a bus when fainting. The bus with the chili perk is able to attack two enemy pets at once and can do serious damage to an early team. It's summoning faint capability, like the sheep, works well with a good number of other pets.
Level 1 Faint: Summon one 5/5 Bus with chili.
Level 2 Faint: Summon one 10/10 Bus with chili
Level 3 Faint: Summon one 15/15 Bus with chili.


7. Camel (Tier 3). The camel takes 7 on the list. It is a Tier 3 that can be very strong and integral even in the late game. As the camel gets hurt, it buffs the friendly pet behind it. If you can get a tanky camel in the front of your team, your second pet can become a powerhouse!
Level 1: Hurt: Give the nearest friend behind +2 Attack and +2 Health.
Level 2: Hurt: Give the nearest friend behind +4 Attack and +4 Health.
Level 3: Hurt: Give the nearest friend behind +6 Attack and +6 Health.


6. Peacock (Tier 2). As only a tier 2 pet, the peacock is very good. Its attack power increases as it gets hurt. If paired with a supporting pet can buff the peacock's health, the peacock can become an absolute menance to the enemy team.
Level 1: Hurt: Gain +4 Attack.
Level 2: Hurt: Gain +8 Attack.
Level 3: Hurt: Gain +12 Attack.


5. Tiger (Tier 6). The tiger is an absolute crowd favorite and icon of the game itself. It works well practically on any team which has space for it. Since it make the pet in front of it repeat their ability times, put behind a key pet, your time will be twice as good in a sense.
Level 1: The friend ahead repeats their ability in battle as if they were level 1.
Level 2: The friend ahead repeats their ability in battle as if they were level 2.
Level 3:The friend ahead repeats their ability in battle as if they were level 3.


4. Guineafowl (Tier 2). The Guineafowl is the most controversial pet I have on my list. With the right team, it can become one of the most broken pets in the game. Its mechanic is that it earns trumpets everytime it gets hurt and the trumpets get used to summon a golden retrieve with the attack and health equal to the number of trumpets. If get a guineafowl that can take a lot of hits, you're set to win the game.
Level 1: Hurt: Gain +2 Trumpet.
Level 2: Hurt: Gain +4 Trumpet.
Level 3: Hurt: Gain +6 Trumpet.


3. Snake (Tier 6). The Snake is my third faveorite pet in the game. It attacks a random pet additionally whenver the pet infront of it attacks. It makes a great addition to many team comps and often becomes the main winning factor for Super Auto Pet Battles many battles.
Level 1: Friend ahead attacks: Deal 5 Damage to one random enemy.
Level 2: Friend ahead attacks: Deal 10 Damage to one random enemy.
Level 3: Friend ahead attacks: Deal 15 Damage to one random enemy.


9. Monkey (Tier 5). The secret to winning in Super Auto Pets is to make the pets on your team very strong. Many pets are able to do this in some round about fasion but none do this better than the monkey (with maybe the exception of my number 1 pick)!
Level 1: End turn: Give right-most friend +2 Attack and +3 Health.
Level 2: End turn: Give right-most friend +4 Attack and +6 Health.
Level 3:End turn: Give right-most friend +6 Attack and +9 Health.


1. Cat (Tier 6). The number one pet goes to what I think is the best pet in the game and that is the cat!! So far, food items have not been mentioned in this list, but what food items do is they either give a pet a perk or give them additional health and attack. What the cat does is it scales the amount of health and atttack given from food items. This allows your pets to become insanely tanky and powerful in arguably the most efficient manner built in the game.
Level 1:Food gives double health and attack. Works 2 times per turn.
Level 2: Food gives triple health and attack. Works 2 times per turn.
Level 3: Food gives quadruple health and attack. Works 2 times per turn.
